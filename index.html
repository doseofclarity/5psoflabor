The 5 P's of Labor – Adele Pillitteri, 7e

1. Passage (Maternal Bony Pelvis & Soft Tissues)

The passage includes the bony pelvis (inlet, midpelvis, outlet) and the soft tissues (cervix, vagina, perineum). Pelvic adequacy is essential for vaginal birth.

Pelvis Types (Caldwell‑Moloy)
  • Gynecoid (female) – ideal, round inlet, well‑curved sacrum.
  • Android (male) – heart‑shaped, narrow subpubic arch; associated with prolonged labor.
  • Anthropoid – long AP, narrow transverse; favorable for occiput posterior.
  • Platypelloid – flat, shallow; rare.
Critical Diameters
  • Pelvic inlet: transverse (13 cm) and AP (11 cm). Smallest AP = obstetrical conjugate (≥10 cm).
  • Midpelvis: ischial spines (station 0); interspinous diameter (≥10 cm).
  • Outlet: intertuberous diameter (≥11 cm) and pubic arch (>90°).
📏 Clinical relevance: Cephalopelvic disproportion (CPD) occurs when the passage is too small or passenger too large → obstructed labor → cesarean.

2. Passenger (Fetus)

Factors: fetal head (skull), attitude, lie, presentation, position, and station.

💀 Fetal Skull
Sutures (sagittal, coronal, lambdoid) allow molding; fontanelles (anterior closes 12–18 mo, posterior at 2 mo). Biparietal diameter (9.25 cm) is smallest transverse.
🔄 Attitude (flexion)
Complete flexion = chin to chest (suboccipitobregmatic, 9.5 cm) – most favorable. Extension increases diameter (occipitofrontal 12 cm, occipitomental 13.5 cm).
⬇️ Lie & Presentation
Longitudinal (95%) vs transverse. Cephalic (vertex) is normal; breech (3%) or shoulder (rare).
📍 Position & Station
Position = relation of fetal occiput to maternal pelvis (LOA most common). Station = level of presenting part relative to ischial spines (0 engaged; -4 to +4).
Pillitteri note: Molding and caput succedaneum are normal adaptations; cephalohematoma (bleeding under periosteum) resolves slowly.

3. Powers (Uterine Contractions + Maternal Pushing)

Primary powers: involuntary uterine contractions → effacement and dilation.
Secondary powers: maternal bearing‑down (pushing) after full dilation.

📈 Contraction characteristics
Frequency (start to start), duration (seconds), intensity (mild/moderate/strong). Adequate labor: 3–5 contractions/10 min, lasting 45–60 sec, strong by active phase.
🔄 Effacement & Dilation
Effacement = cervical thinning (0–100%). Primiparas: effacement before dilation; multiparas: occur together. Dilation = 0–10 cm.
⚡ Mnemonic "POWER": P – progressive, O – organized waves, W – work of contraction (increment/acme/decrement), E – effacement, R – regular rhythm.

4. Maternal Position

Maternal positioning affects uterine blood flow, pelvic diameters, descent, and maternal comfort.

✅ Upright/ambulation
Gravity aids descent; reduces need for oxytocin; shorter first stage. Avoid supine hypotension.
✅ Lateral (left side‑lying)
Optimal uteroplacental perfusion; reduces aortocaval compression; useful for fetal distress.
✅ Squatting / kneeling
Increases pelvic outlet diameter (by 1–2 cm); effective for second stage.
⚠️ Supine lithotomy
Traditional but may impede descent; risk of supine hypotensive syndrome; limited pelvic expansion.
Evidence‑based: Frequent position changes improve labor outcomes and reduce perineal trauma.

5. Psyche (Psychological State)

Maternal anxiety, fear, cultural beliefs, support system, and preparation directly influence labor progress and pain perception.

  • Stress → catecholamines → inhibit uterine contractility, reduce placental perfusion, prolong labor.
  • Continuous labor support (doula/partner) → decreased need for analgesia, shorter labor, fewer cesareans.
  • Childbirth education (Lamaze, Bradley) → increases self‑efficacy, reduces fear‑tension‑pain cycle.
  • Cultural and language barriers → may increase stress; nurse should provide individualized, respectful care.
Nursing intervention: Explain procedures, offer encouragement, advocate for birth preferences, and reduce environmental stressors.

Mnemonics & Clinical Pearls

🧠 5 P's Recall
Passage, Passenger, Powers, Position, Psyche.
📏 Pelvis Types (Caldwell‑Moloy)
"GAP" – Gynecoid (ideal), Android (male), Platypelloid (flat). Anthropoid is "APE" – AP elongated.
🔄 Fetal Attitude Mnemonic "FLEX"
F – full flexion (9.5 cm), L – less flexion (military 12 cm), E – extended brow (13 cm), X – extreme hyperextension (face, 13.5 cm).
📍 Station
"‑3 to +3" – minus above spines, plus below spines. Crowning = station +4/+5.
⚡ Contraction Assessment (3 D's)
Duration (seconds), Degree (intensity by palpation), Deflection (frequency).
💊 Powers vs Passenger
If powers are adequate but no descent → suspect passenger/passage problem (CPD, malposition).
